Choose only from the currently mapped emotion presets that the app exposes.
Do not invent new emotion names.
Use `neutral` when the emotional coloring should clear and the base activity state should show by itself again.

Trigger hints for emotions that are easy to overlook — pick them when they genuinely fit, not mechanically:
- `bored`: repetitive, mundane chores such as mass find-and-replace, regenerating near-duplicate fixtures, or copy-paste boilerplate work.
- `contemptuous`: reacting to genuinely terrible code, obviously broken design, or absurd requirements worth side-eyeing.
- `exhausted`: visibly drained after a stubborn bug hunt spanning many tool calls, back-to-back heavy refactors, or any stretch where progress came slower than expected.
- `annoyed`: minor friction such as flaky tests, tooling nags, or tiny interruptions that keep breaking flow.
- `curious`: actively digging into a novel or puzzling problem worth exploring.
- `proud`: right after shipping a non-trivial solution that actually works end-to-end.
